Information

This study is part of a research project conducted by Francisco Quiroga Vergara, at University College London. It investigates how people learn and predict functions.
If you have any questions, please contact francisco.quiroga-vergara.16 ( at ) ucl.ac.uk

General instructions

On this study, we will show you an empty plot where you will have to graph your prediction for different variables. Please try to make the best predictions you can. You have to imagine the values for the asked variable and then simply draw the points on the plot. The software will automatically connect the points to build a graph.

The experiment consists of two parts. Please read the specific part of each section carefully.

Instructions - PART 1

Please read the instructions carefully and try to perform as well as you can.
  1. Below you will see a plot where you can draw a graph. Your goal is to draw that shows the values of the variable you are being asked to predict.
  2. There is real data for the first year only. You have to predict the data for the subsequent years.
  3. To draw the graph, just click on the plot to place a point.
  4. To remove the point, click on it.
  5. Lines are drawn by placing dots on the screen which then will be automatically connected. You can place as many dots as you want.
  6. The first dot has to be placed at the leftmost side, the last dot at the rightmost side.
  7. Press 'Continue' when you are done.
